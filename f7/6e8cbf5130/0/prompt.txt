We decided to use the *Swin UNETR* (Swin Vision Transformer) backbone, specifically initialized with MONAI's Self-Supervised Learning (SSL) pre-trained weights (model_swinvit.pt).

Here is exactly why this is a massive advantage over standard models:

* *True 3D Transformer:* Unlike standard ResNets that use localized 3D convolutions, the Swin ViT uses a hierarchical transformer architecture. It divides the 3D brain into patches and uses self-attention to understand the global structure of the brain across long distances.
* *Self-Supervised Pre-training:* These specific weights weren't just trained to classify diseases. They were trained on thousands of unlabeled 3D medical scans using proxy tasks (like hiding patches of a brain and forcing the model to reconstruct them, or rotating the brain and making the model guess the angle).
* *Modality Agnostic:* Because it was pre-trained using structural reconstruction tasks rather than pixel-perfect matching, it is incredible at understanding human anatomy regardless of whether the scan is a T1 or T2.

By loading these weights into a shared encoder (Siamese network), your pipeline is essentially starting the hackathon with a model that already has a PhD in 3D brain geometry, and your contrastive training loop is just teaching it how to compare two different scans.
